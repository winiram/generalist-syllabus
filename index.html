An Alternative Syllabus for Humans

Most of what we were taught reflects a narrow slice of human knowledge — Western, patriarchal, built to serve a particular kind of power. This syllabus is a deliberate correction: ancient philosophy alongside tech criticism, mystical poetry alongside feminist theory, the Stoics alongside Thich Nhat Hanh. The goal is not to finish it. The goal is to become harder to fool — including by yourself.

Nine Modules

01

The Mind That Listens — Music, Cognition & Consciousness

Psychology · Philosophy · Hindustani Classical
You have lived inside Hindustani classical music your whole life. This module asks: what is actually happening — neurologically, philosophically, spiritually — when you listen, practice, or improvise? It takes your existing knowledge seriously as a site of inquiry.
Book
This Is Your Brain on Music
Daniel J. Levitin
A neuroscientist and former record producer explains how music is processed in the brain. Read it critically — his examples are Western-centric, but the cognitive science is solid. Ask: what does he miss about raga grammar and time cycles?
Book
Music and the Mind
Anthony Storr
A Jungian psychiatrist examines why music matters so profoundly to human experience. Explores music as a developmental, emotional, and spiritual necessity — not mere entertainment.
Book
The Ragas of North India
Walter Kaufmann
Dense and encyclopedic. Use it as a reference alongside your practice — seeing the structure you already know described analytically can be revelatory.
Paper
Music and Emotion: Theory and Research
Juslin & Sloboda (eds.) — Oxford University Press, 2001
A foundational anthology. The chapter on "emotional contagion" is particularly striking — it explains why a certain alap can feel like grief you didn't know you had.
Paper
Aesthetic Emotion and Music (Rasa Theory)
Various — search JSTOR: "raga rasa affect"
The literature on how ragas embody emotional and temporal states (rasa theory) connects directly to both aesthetics and psychology. Bharata Muni's Natyashastra predates Western affective science by two millennia.
Video
Music and the Brain — Robert Zatorre (Montreal Neurological Institute)
YouTube · Royal Institution lecture
Zatorre's research on music and dopamine is transformative. His experiments on "chills" during music will change how you hear your own reactions to a particularly beautiful meend.
Video
The Neuroscience of Music — TED-Ed
YouTube · TED-Ed
A tight, accessible 5-minute overview of what happens in the brain during musical listening — useful primer before Zatorre's longer lecture.
Music
Raga Yaman — Pandit Bhimsen Joshi (Live)
YouTube · ITC Sangeet Research Academy archival
Listen not as a student, but as an analyst. Where does time dilate? Where does the raga breathe? Pair with your reading on music cognition and notice what theory can and cannot explain.
Website
Music & Science (SAGE open-access journal)
musicandscience.net
Peer-reviewed, fully open-access journal covering music cognition, emotion, and neuroscience. A reliable source for going deeper after the books.
02

The Examined Self — Psychology, Spirituality & Sacred Texts

Psychology · Contemplative Practice · Indian Scriptures · Sufi Poetry · Mysticism · Buddhist Practice · Stoicism · Poetry
You live between frameworks — the rationalist, quantified culture of Silicon Valley and a tradition of spiritual practice that refuses to be measured. This module refuses to resolve that tension. It sits inside it. It moves from rigorous psychological inquiry into the texts humans have reached for when ordinary language fails — when grief, joy, longing, or awe exceeds what prose can hold. Read slowly. Return often.
Book
The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali
Swami Satchidananda (trans. & commentary)
The classical psychology of the Indian tradition. Citta vritti — fluctuations of the mind — is more rigorous as a cognitive framework than many modern accounts. Read alongside contemporary psychology.
Book
The Yoga Vasishtha
Attributed to Valmiki · Best edition: Vasistha's Yoga, trans. Swami Venkatesananda (SUNY Press) · Free abridged text on Archive.org
A discourse on consciousness, time, and the nature of the world as dream — Advaita Vedanta at its most expansive and poetic. Do not read it sequentially. Open it anywhere.
Book
The Bhagavatam (Srimad Bhagavatam)
Trans. various · Recommended: C. L. Goswami & M. A. Shastri abridged edition
Twelve books of cosmology, mythology, and devotional philosophy. Start with the Tenth Book — Krishna's life, narrated with lyrical intensity unmatched in Sanskrit literature. Not a text you master; one you grow into.
Book
The Bhagavad Gita
Trans. various · Recommended: Barbara Stoler Miller (Bantam) for poetry; Georg Feuerstein for scholarship · Free text at sacred-texts.com
Eighteen chapters on duty, action, and the nature of the self — delivered on a battlefield to a man paralysed by an impossible choice. Not a book to agree or disagree with. A book to argue with, for a lifetime.
Book
Burn Down Your House: Provocations from Kabir
Shabnam Virmani · Speaking Tiger Books, 2024
Virmani's two-decade journey with Kabir's poetry — across Bhakti singers, oral traditions, and Sufi communities. Kabir was a 15th-century weaver-poet who refused every religious boundary. A living commentary on the Sutras.
Book
The Bijak of Kabir
Trans. Linda Hess & Shukdev Singh · Oxford University Press · Free on Archive.org
The most important surviving collection of Kabir's verses — deliberately abrasive, mocking pandits and mullahs alike. The Sakhi couplets read best alongside the Gita: opposite ends of the Indian devotional spectrum.
Book
The Essential Rumi
Trans. Coleman Barks · HarperOne
Barks re-hears Rumi as living voice rather than museum artifact. The poems on longing and the reed flute cut from the reed bed will resonate directly with what you already know about how music carries feeling that language cannot.
Book
Rumi: The Guest House & Other Poems
Trans. Jawid Mojaddedi · Oxford World's Classics
More precise and closer to the Persian than Barks. The "Guest House" poem — treating each difficulty as an honored visitor — is one of the most therapeutically useful pieces of literature in any tradition.
Book
Meister Eckhart: The Essential Sermons, Commentaries, Treatises, and Defense
Trans. Edmund Colledge & Bernard McGinn · Paulist Press
A 13th-century Dominican mystic who taught that beneath all particular being is a groundless ground, a silence prior to all names. His proximity to Advaita Vedanta — which he almost certainly never read — is remarkable.
Book
Meditations
Marcus Aurelius · Free at MIT Classics · Best modern trans: Gregory Hays (Modern Library)
A Roman emperor's private notebook, never intended for publication — daily reminders that most of what troubles us is opinion, not fact. Gregory Hays's translation reads as direct, urgent speech.
Book
Letters from a Stoic (Epistulae Morales)
Seneca · Free on Project Gutenberg · Best trans: Robin Campbell (Penguin Classics)
More personal and warmer than Marcus — a conversation about how to live. On time, friendship, and the fear of death. "On the Shortness of Life" is eight pages that will reorganize your sense of what matters.
Book
The Complete Poems of Emily Dickinson
Emily Dickinson · Free at Poetry Foundation · Best print edition: Thomas H. Johnson (Little, Brown)
Nearly 1,800 poems — some of the most precise writing about consciousness, death, and God in the English language. Start with: "Because I could not stop for Death," "The Brain is wider than the Sky," "Tell all the truth but tell it slant."
Book
How to Love / How to Fight / How to Relax / How to Sit / How to Walk
Thich Nhat Hanh · Parallax Press · The "How To" series
Five slim books, each built around a single practice. How to Love redefines love as understanding. How to Fight is about transforming anger without suppressing it. Begin wherever you are pulled; each is complete in itself.
Book
The Miracle of Mindfulness
Thich Nhat Hanh · Beacon Press
Originally a letter to a fellow monk — it introduced mindfulness to the Western world before the word became an industry. Read this first if you want context; the shorter "How To" books if you want immediate practice.
Book
Waking Up: A Guide to Spirituality Without Religion
Sam Harris
A Western rationalist trying to account for meditative experience without metaphysics. Read Harris, then read the critiques from Buddhist scholars like B. Alan Wallace. The debate is more interesting than either position alone.
Book
The Untethered Soul
Michael A. Singer
Less rigorous than other texts here, but unusually good at articulating the observer-self distinction — the idea that you are the one watching the mind, not the mind itself. A useful bridge between pop-psychology and deeper philosophy.
Book
The Psychology of Religion and Coping
Kenneth I. Pargament
Rigorous empirical psychology of how spiritual practices function as genuine coping mechanisms — not illusion, but psychic events with measurable effects. An important counterweight to secular reductionism.
Paper
Altered States of Consciousness (1969)
Charles Tart — Journal of Transpersonal Psychology
Tart argues that different states of consciousness are qualitatively distinct systems, not simply degrees of wakefulness. A rigorous reframing of what contemplative traditions have always claimed.
Video
How Do You Explain Consciousness? — David Chalmers
TED · 18 min
The best short introduction to why consciousness is philosophically devastating for materialist science. Chalmers frames the "hard problem" with clarity — essential context for engaging seriously with spirituality and psychology.
Podcast
On Being — with Krista Tippett
onbeing.org · Free archive
Start with Bessel van der Kolk on trauma, Robin Wall Kimmerer on indigenous knowledge, and V.V. Raman on science and spirituality. The archive is vast — use search to find the intersections that matter to you.
Website
The Marginalian
Maria Popova · themarginalian.org · Free, reader-supported
The most sustained single-person project curating connections between literature, science, philosophy, and art. Her essays on Dickinson, Rumi, and Marcus Aurelius are among the best entry points to each of those figures.
03

The Machine & The Human — Technology, Society & Power

Sociology of Technology · Tech Criticism · Philosophy of Technology
You work inside the machine. This module helps you see it from the outside — not to leave it, but to understand what you are part of. The best tech criticism is not anti-technology. It is anti-naïve.
Book
The Age of Surveillance Capitalism
Shoshana Zuboff
The definitive sociological account of how behavioral data became the raw material of a new economic logic. Dense and demanding, but repays close reading. This book will permanently change how you see the products you work on.
Book
Invisible Women: Data Bias in a World Designed for Men
Caroline Criado Perez
Meticulously evidenced account of how the default human in data, product design, medicine, and urban planning is male — and the costs of that invisibility. As a woman building tech, this is both professional and personal reading.
Book
Race After Technology
Ruha Benjamin
Introduces the "New Jim Code" — how algorithmic systems reproduce racial inequity under the guise of neutrality. Essential for anyone building products at scale. Connects to questions of whose data, whose values, whose future.
Essay
Do Artifacts Have Politics? (1980)
Langdon Winner — Daedalus, Vol. 109
Eight pages that changed how the humanities thinks about technology. Winner argues that technologies embed political values and power relations — they are never neutral. Read it in one sitting.
Essay
A Cyborg Manifesto (1985)
Donna Haraway — Socialist Review
One of the most important essays of the 20th century. Haraway argues for the cyborg as a figure that refuses the binaries of nature/culture, human/machine, self/other. Transgressive, joyful, and rigorous. Free PDF.
Video
Coded Bias — Documentary (trailer & discussion)
YouTube · Netflix / POV documentary
Joy Buolamwini's investigation into facial recognition bias. Her research showing dramatically lower accuracy for darker-skinned women is landmark work. Pair with her TED talk (free).
Podcast
Tech Won't Save Us — Paris Marx
techwontsave.us · Free
Consistently rigorous left-critical tech analysis. Best episodes: Safiya Umoja Noble (search bias), Kate Crawford (AI and power), Ben Tarnoff (tech and labor). Not ideologically monolithic — guests genuinely disagree.
Website
Algorithmic Justice League
ajl.org · Founded by Joy Buolamwini
Research, advocacy, and accessible explainers on bias in AI systems. Bridges academic research and activist practice. A resource you'll return to frequently.
Website
Data & Society Research Library
datasociety.net · Open access
Freely downloadable reports on the social implications of data-centric technologies. One of the most reliable sources for current, rigorous tech-society research.
Book
Staying with the Trouble: Making Kin in the Chthulucene
Donna Haraway · Duke University Press, 2016
Haraway argues against both techno-optimism and despair — proposing instead that we learn to live inside the damage without either fixing it away or giving up. Her concept of "making kin" across species, disciplines, and worldviews is one of the most generative ideas in contemporary theory. Pair with Braiding Sweetgrass in Module 06.
04

Feminist Thought & The Life We Actually Live

Feminism · Intersectionality · Philosophy · Sociology
Feminist theory at its best is not a political position — it is an epistemology. It asks: who gets to produce knowledge, about whom, for whose benefit? This module moves from foundational texts to the questions closest to your own life.
Book
Feminist Theory: From Margin to Center
bell hooks
hooks argues that feminist theory built from the experience of the most marginalized women produces better, more truthful theory. As someone who navigates multiple margins simultaneously, this framing will feel clarifying.
Book
Feminism Without Borders
Chandra Talpade Mohanty
An Indian feminist scholar critiques Western feminism's tendency to construct a monolithic "Third World Woman" and argues for a genuinely transnational feminism. As a South Indian woman who has lived both contexts, her analysis will resonate specifically.
Book
The Second Sex
Simone de Beauvoir
Don't try to read it all. Start with the introduction ("One is not born, but rather becomes, a woman") and Part IV. De Beauvoir's framework of woman as "Other" prefigures everything from Haraway to Criado Perez.
Essay
Mapping the Margins: Intersectionality, Identity Politics, and Violence Against Women of Color (1991)
Kimberlé Crenshaw — Stanford Law Review · Free PDF
The essay that introduced intersectionality as an analytical framework. Crenshaw shows how Black women fall through the gaps between race-discrimination law and sex-discrimination law. Foundational. Free online.
Video
The Urgency of Intersectionality — Kimberlé Crenshaw
TED · 19 min · Free
Crenshaw makes her academic framework visceral and immediate through real case studies. One of the best TED talks ever made.
Book
Brotopia: Breaking Up the Boys' Club of Silicon Valley
Emily Chang
A reported account of Silicon Valley's culture of sexism. Read as sociology alongside the theoretical texts — the names and companies will be familiar. The distance between theory and your lived experience will shrink considerably.
Podcast
Feminist Frequency Radio
Free on all podcast platforms
Focused on gender representation in media and technology. Anita Sarkeesian's work on women in games is a gateway to broader questions about whose stories get told and how.
05

Identity, Diaspora & the Translated Self

Postcolonial Theory · Diaspora Studies · Philosophy of Identity
You crossed an ocean in 2014 and carry at least two cultures in your nervous system. This module is about what that means — philosophically, psychologically, politically — and what unique forms of knowledge it produces.
Book
The Location of Culture
Homi K. Bhabha
Bhabha's concept of "the third space" — the hybrid space between cultures that migrants inhabit — is one of the most useful frameworks for understanding your own experience. Dense, but worth the effort.
Book
The Namesake
Jhumpa Lahiri
Fiction as phenomenology. Lahiri renders the interior experience of South Asian diaspora — the grief and freedom of translation — more precisely than most academic texts. Read it as philosophy, not as comfort.
Book
Orientalism
Edward Said
Said's account of how the West constructed "the East" as its exotic, irrational other — and how that construction served imperial power. Read the introduction and Part I at minimum. Then notice where you have unconsciously adopted the Western gaze toward your own culture.
Book
The Wretched of the Earth
Frantz Fanon
Fanon on the psychological violence of colonialism — how it is internalized, reproduced, and resisted. Chapter 5 is a devastating account of how political structures damage psyches. Foundational for understanding South Asia's modern history.
Essay
Can the Subaltern Speak? (1988)
Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak · Free via Archive.org
Spivak asks whether those systematically silenced by colonialism can make themselves heard — and on whose terms. Difficult, important, and unexpectedly personal once you sit with it.
Podcast
Desi Standard Time
Spotify · Free
Thoughtful conversations about South Asian diaspora identity — negotiating tradition and modernity, caste, queerness, mental health. Less academic than the readings above, but an important voice from inside the community.
Podcast
Brown Girl Therapy Podcast
browngirltherapy.com · Free
Sangeeta Pillai and Sahaj Kohli on mental health for South Asian women — covering intergenerational trauma, family dynamics, identity, and the particular exhaustion of being a high-achieving immigrant woman. Grounded and genuine.
Book
Aflame: Learning from Silence
Pico Iyer · 2024
Iyer — born in Oxford to Indian parents, raised in California, living in rural Japan for nearly four decades — has made a life's work of inhabiting the spaces between cultures. Aflame is his most interior book: a meditation on silence, monastic life, and what it means to be lit from within rather than consumed. For someone who practices Hindustani classical music and navigates Silicon Valley, Iyer's particular stillness feels like a road less taken — and worth knowing exists.
Book
The Half Known Life: In Search of Paradise
Pico Iyer · 2023
Iyer travels from Iran to North Korea to the Dalai Lama's Himalayas to Japan's temples, asking where paradise might be found — and whether the search itself is the point. The book arrives at a quietly radical conclusion: that peace is not a destination but a way of attending to what's already here. Beautifully written, quietly devastating.
Book
Autumn Light: Season of Fire and Farewells
Pico Iyer · 2019
A meditation on impermanence, aging parents, and marriage — set against the backdrop of Japan's autumn maple season. Iyer's great subject is how to hold on to what matters while knowing everything falls away. The book asks the question Japan asks every autumn: how do you love something precisely because it is transient? A natural companion to the Thich Nhat Hanh books in Module 09.
06

How to Think — Philosophy as Practice

Philosophy · Epistemology · Ethics · Cognitive Linguistics
This module is less about philosophy's content and more about what philosophy does — how it sharpens thinking, surfaces hidden assumptions, and gives you tools to interrogate ideas rather than just receive them. These are books you return to, not books you finish.
Book
Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid
Douglas Hofstadter
A Pulitzer Prize-winning exploration of self-reference, consciousness, and the nature of mind — through mathematics, music, and visual art. For someone with your combination of interests, this is essential. Start with the dialogues between Achilles and the Tortoise.
Book
Braiding Sweetgrass
Robin Wall Kimmerer
A botanist and Potawatomi Nation member argues that Western science and indigenous epistemologies illuminate different truths — and that we need both. Deeply relevant to the spirituality-science tension running through your interests.
Book
Women, Fire, and Dangerous Things
George Lakoff
Lakoff's cognitive linguistics shows that the categories we think in are not neutral — they are embodied, culturally shaped, and ideologically loaded. How we categorize is how we see. Essential foundation for both tech ethics and feminist theory.
Book
An Illustrated Book of Bad Arguments
Ali Almossawi · Full text free at bookofbadarguments.com
Nineteen common logical fallacies — straw man, false dilemma, ad hominem, slippery slope — illustrated with memorable animal characters and clear explanations. Deceptively simple and immediately useful. Read it once, then keep it as a field guide for spotting bad reasoning in product meetings, online debates, and your own internal arguments.
Book
Sophie's World
Jostein Gaarder
A novel that is also a history of Western philosophy — from Socrates to Sartre with unusual elegance. An ideal map before the territory if you haven't had a formal philosophy background.
Podcast
Philosophy Bites
philosophybites.com · Free archive
Short (15–20 min), rigorous interviews with major philosophers on single topics. Start with: personal identity (Derek Parfit), moral luck (Thomas Nagel), and the epistemology of testimony. Vast free archive.
Podcast
The Partially Examined Life
partiallyexaminedlife.com · Free episodes
Philosophers reading and debating primary texts together — with humor and genuine disagreement. A good way to encounter Hegel, Wittgenstein, or Nietzsche without the isolation of reading alone.
Website
Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
plato.stanford.edu · Free
The gold standard reference for philosophical topics — peer-reviewed, rigorously maintained, completely free. Any time a philosopher or concept confuses you, start here before Wikipedia.
Behavioral Economics — a sub-section Classical economics assumed humans are rational actors who optimize for self-interest. Behavioral economics shows we are not — and maps exactly how we aren't. This is not merely an academic point. Understanding cognitive bias is self-defense: against manipulative product design, financial decisions made under stress, and the stories we tell ourselves. These three books form the essential core.
Book
Thinking, Fast and Slow
Daniel Kahneman
A Nobel laureate's account of the two systems governing human thought: System 1 (fast, intuitive) and System 2 (slow, deliberate). Kahneman maps cognitive biases — anchoring, loss aversion, the halo effect — with precision and memorable examples. This is the book that makes you realize how much of what feels like reasoning is actually post-hoc rationalization of gut instinct.
Book
Nudge: The Final Edition
Richard Thaler & Cass Sunstein
Thaler and Sunstein introduce "choice architecture" — the idea that how options are presented shapes what people choose, regardless of their stated preferences. The word "nudge" has entered mainstream policy and product vocabulary because the insight is so verifiable. The entire field of UX dark patterns is choice architecture deployed against users rather than for them.
Book
Predictably Irrational
Dan Ariely
The most accessible entry point into behavioral economics — each chapter tackles a specific irrationality: why "free" is so psychologically powerful, why expectations shape experience, why we value things more once we own them. Read this first if Kahneman feels too dense — it will make you want to go back to the harder book.
Book
Misbehaving: The Making of Behavioral Economics
Richard Thaler
Thaler recounts how behavioral economics fought its way into mainstream economics against fierce resistance from rationalist orthodoxy — a reminder that paradigm shifts require not just good evidence but sustained institutional struggle. The book is also quietly funny: his account of being condescended to for pointing out that humans don't behave like textbook models is a study in epistemic stubbornness.
07

Synthesis — The Threads That Run Through Everything

Cross-disciplinary · Capstone Reading
These texts refuse to stay in one discipline. They are for after you've worked through the other modules — but many are good entry points too.
Book
The Structure of Scientific Revolutions
Thomas Kuhn
Kuhn's concept of "paradigm shifts" — that science doesn't progress linearly but through revolutionary breaks — applies to every field in this syllabus. Essential for understanding why it is so hard to think outside the frameworks you were trained in.
Book
Ways of Seeing
John Berger · Full text available online
Starts from painting, ends at advertising, and rewires how you look at images, power, and gender in between. "Men act, women appear" — four decades old and hasn't aged a day.
Book
Atlas of AI
Kate Crawford
Crawford maps the full material and political infrastructure behind AI — from lithium mines to data center workers to military contracts. The counterpart to Zuboff: where Zuboff is about data as capital, Crawford is about AI as empire.
Book
The Dispossessed
Ursula K. Le Guin
Science fiction as philosophy. A physicist moves between two societies built on opposing values — property vs. radical mutual aid. Le Guin does ethics through worldbuilding with more sophistication than most moral philosophy. Read this one slowly.
Website
Aeon Magazine
aeon.co · Free
The best general-readership home for rigorous ideas across philosophy, psychology, science, and culture. Free, browser-based, searchable. A reliable source of quality long-form essays across almost every topic in this syllabus.
Website
The Marginalian
Maria Popova — themarginalian.org · Free
Popova curates connections between literature, science, philosophy, and art with unusual intelligence. Use as a weekly supplement — her essay archives on consciousness, creativity, and meaning are genuinely rich.
08

Money, Power & Financial Literacy

Personal Finance · Investing · Women & Wealth · The Psychology of Money
Financial literacy is not a neutral skill — it is a form of power, and women have been systematically excluded from it. This module treats money not just as a practical matter but as a philosophical and feminist one: who controls capital, who builds wealth, and what does financial independence actually make possible?
Book
The Psychology of Money
Morgan Housel
The best entry point into financial thinking for someone who is more interested in ideas than spreadsheets. Housel argues that financial success has less to do with knowledge and more to do with behavior, patience, and knowing yourself. Connects directly to the psychology modules — the chapters on ego, greed, and the "moving goalposts" of enough are essential reading.
Book
Clever Girl Finance: Ditch Debt, Save Money and Build Real Wealth
Bola Sokunbi
Written by a Nigerian-American woman who noticed that almost no financial books were written for women of color. Covers budgeting, debt, saving, and investing with a warm, non-judgmental tone. Practical and empowering — pairs naturally with the feminist theory in Module 04. The accompanying website, clevergirlfinance.com, is a free ongoing resource.
Book
I Will Teach You to Be Rich
Ramit Sethi
A blunt, practical 6-week program for automating savings, optimizing credit, and investing without guilt. His emphasis on building systems rather than relying on willpower is exactly the right frame for a busy professional. Updated second edition covers high-cost-of-living cities and current realities.
Book
Your Money or Your Life
Vicki Robin & Joe Dominguez
A classic that reframes money as "life energy" — the hours of your finite existence exchanged for it. Asks the uncomfortable question: is what you're spending your life on actually worth it? Unusually philosophical for a personal finance book, and a natural companion to the spirituality and diaspora modules — all three ask versions of the same question about what a good life requires.
Book
We Should All Be Millionaires
Rachel Rodgers
A Black woman lawyer-turned-entrepreneur argues that wealth is not just a personal goal but a feminist and political one — that women building financial power changes what is possible for their families, communities, and society. Confronts the cultural conditioning that makes many women, especially immigrant and women of color, ambivalent about wealth-building.
Book
The Simple Path to Wealth
JL Collins
The clearest, most jargon-free account of index fund investing available. Collins argues for radical simplicity: one or two funds, long time horizon, leave it alone. Originally written as a series of letters to his daughter. An antidote to the complexity the financial industry manufactures to justify its fees.
Book
Pound Foolish: Exposing the Dark Side of the Personal Finance Industry
Helaine Olen
A financial journalist dismantles the self-help finance industry — showing how much of it profits from anxiety rather than solving it, and how systemic economic inequality cannot be fixed by individual behavior change alone. Essential critical counterweight to the actionable books above. Read this alongside Rodgers and Collins to hold both truths at once.
Website
Clever Girl Finance
clevergirlfinance.com · Free
Free courses, calculators, and articles on every aspect of personal finance for women. One of the most substantive free financial education resources available — not ad-heavy clickbait but genuinely useful content.
Podcast
Afford Anything — Paula Pant
affordanything.com · Free
Paula Pant's core thesis: you can afford anything, but not everything — so choose deliberately. Her interviews with economists, investors, and psychologists are unusually rigorous for a personal finance podcast. Particularly good on the financial independence / early retirement movement and its philosophical implications.
09

Caste, Race & the Architecture of Inequality

Anti-caste Thought · Dalit Writing · Race Theory · Structural Inequality
Caste and race are not feelings. They are architectures — systems of enforced hierarchy that organise who gets to live where, learn what, earn how much, and be seen as fully human. This module reads them together without collapsing their differences. For a South Indian woman living in Silicon Valley — where caste operates quietly in hiring, networks, and community — this is among the most personally urgent material in the syllabus.
Book
Annihilation of Caste
B.R. Ambedkar · 1936 · Free on Project Gutenberg
The foundational anti-caste text — a speech Ambedkar prepared for a Hindu reform conference that then disinvited him for being too radical. It argues that caste cannot be reformed, only annihilated, because it is not a social practice but a religious doctrine. Ambedkar's argument is careful, devastating, and still largely unrefuted. The most important Indian political text of the 20th century that most school curricula refuse to teach.
Book
Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents
Isabel Wilkerson
A Pulitzer Prize-winning journalist argues that what America calls "race" is in fact a caste system — an artificial hierarchy enforced through law, violence, and social ritual. Wilkerson draws parallels between the American racial order, India's caste system, and Nazi Germany, identifying eight shared "pillars of caste." Connects the hierarchies you grew up inside with the one you now inhabit professionally.
Book
Caste Matters
Suraj Yengde
Yengde — a first-generation Dalit scholar with affiliations at Harvard and Oxford — writes from inside the system he anatomizes: the daily humiliations of Dalit life, the glass ceiling in institutions, and the dynamics of tokenism within the Dalit community. His work explicitly connects caste to Silicon Valley, tracing how upper-caste networks travel with migration under the name of "merit."
Book
Coming Out as Dalit
Yashica Dutt
A journalist recounts spending years passing as upper caste in Delhi's media world and then in New York — until she came out publicly and her life changed. The memoir captures what theoretical texts cannot: the psychological cost of concealment and what it takes to reclaim an identity that has been weaponised against you.
Book
The Doctor and the Saint (Introduction to Annihilation of Caste)
Arundhati Roy · Verso Books, 2017
Roy's 179-page introduction to the Verso edition of Annihilation of Caste traces the relationship and rupture between Gandhi and Ambedkar, arguing that Gandhi's version of caste reform was designed to preserve Hindu social order while appearing to challenge it. Her reading of Gandhi is controversial, but the argument is meticulous — and it reframes the entire independence movement from the perspective of those for whom it did not mean liberation.
Book
Why I Am Not a Hindu
Kancha Ilaiah Shepherd
A political scientist from a shepherd-caste background argues that Dalits and OBCs don't share the cultural world of upper-caste Hinduism — that they have distinct foodways, work cultures, and relationships to nature and death. A provocative reading of caste not as hierarchy within a shared culture, but as the coexistence of fundamentally different civilizations.
Book
The New Jim Crow: Mass Incarceration in the Age of Colorblindness
Michelle Alexander
Alexander argues that the US mass incarceration system functions as a racial caste system — a mechanism for social control of Black Americans that reconstituted itself after the formal end of Jim Crow segregation. Her legal analysis is rigorous, her prose is urgent. Read alongside Wilkerson and Ruha Benjamin (Module 03) to see the three layers of the same structure: historical hierarchy, contemporary algorithm, and punitive state apparatus.
Essay
The Case for Reparations
Ta-Nehisi Coates · The Atlantic, 2014 · Free online
One of the most important pieces of American journalism of the 21st century. Coates traces the specific mechanisms — redlining, contract buying, predatory lending — by which Black wealth was systematically extracted over generations. The argument for reparations is not about guilt but about accounting: what was taken, when, by whom, and what remains.

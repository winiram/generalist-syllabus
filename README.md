
markdown# An Alternative Syllabus — On Becoming More Fully Yourself

A self-directed curriculum for a lifelong learner across psychology, philosophy, technology, music, spirituality, and feminism.

**[View the syllabus →](https://winiram.github.io/my-syllabus)**

---

## About

This is a personal reading and learning curriculum — not organized by discipline, but by questions. It is built for someone who sits at an unusual intersection: a South Indian woman with a lifelong practice in Hindustani classical music, a graduate education at UC Berkeley, and over a decade working in Silicon Valley tech.

The syllabus refuses the typical boundaries between fields. A paper on music cognition sits next to rasa theory. Feminist philosophy sits next to postcolonial theory. Surveillance capitalism sits next to Heidegger. The goal is not coverage — it is connection.

## Suggest a Resource

This syllabus is a living document. There is a submission form at the bottom of the page for anyone who wants to suggest a book, paper, podcast, or piece of music. Suggestions that sit at unexpected intersections — or that come from outside the Western canon — are especially welcome.

---

## How to Use This

A syllabus implies sequence, but this kind of learning doesn't work that way. Follow what pulls you. If one text stops you cold, move to another. If a citation catches your eye, follow it. The goal is not completion — it is the development of a mind that finds connections everywhere it looks.
